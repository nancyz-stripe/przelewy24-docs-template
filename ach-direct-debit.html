ACH Direct Debit payments

Accept payments by debiting your US customers' bank accounts.

Overview

ACH Direct Debit lets you collect payments directly from a customer's US bank account. It's one of the most popular payment methods in the United States for recurring and high-value transactions due to its low cost and broad reach across virtually all US bank accounts.

Payment method family Bank debit
Usability Reusable
Reusable payment methods can be saved to a customer for future payments. After the initial setup, customers don't need to re-enter their payment details for subsequent transactions. Learn more
Access type Instant provisional access
Instant provisional access allows you start accepting payments with this method right away. See the eligibility section for more details.
Payment confirmation timing Delayed
Delayed notification payment methods take time to confirm whether the payment was successful. PaymentIntent status changes to processing and then eventually to succeeded or requires_payment_method. You shouldn't fulfill orders until the payment status reaches succeeded. Learn more

Refunds

ACH Direct Debit supports full and partial refunds, and you can issue multiple refunds against a single payment as long as the total refunded amount doesn't exceed the original charge. You have up to 180 days from the original payment to submit a refund through Stripe. Refunds require a minimum of 3 business days to process.

How refunds work

Submit refunds using the Dashboard or the Refunds API. When you submit a refund, Stripe sends the request through the ACH network to the customer's bank. The bank credits the refund to the customer's original bank account.

Refunds for ACH Direct Debit payments that haven't completed yet (status: processing) can't be submitted until the payment succeeds. If a payment fails, no refund is necessary since the funds were never collected.

Track refund status

Check the status of a refund in the Dashboard on the payment details page, or through the Refunds API. Refunds can remain in a pending state while the bank processes the request.

Stripe sends webhook events for refund status changes:

  • refund.updated — The refund succeeded or its status changed.
  • refund.failed — The refund failed. Check the failure_reason field on the Refund object for details.

Disputes

Customers can dispute ACH Direct Debit payments through their bank. Dispute windows vary by account type: personal account holders have up to 60 calendar days from the statement date, while business account holders have 2 business days.

ACH disputes are final and cannot be appealed. When a customer disputes a payment, the funds are withdrawn from your Stripe balance and the associated mandate is invalidated — you'll need to collect a new mandate before debiting that customer again.

Reduce disputes

To minimize disputes, use a recognizable statement descriptor and send payment notification emails so customers can identify the charge. Proactively refunding disputed transactions can also help, since refunds resolve the dispute without additional fees.

Mandates

To debit a customer's bank account with ACH, you must first obtain their authorization through a mandate. A mandate is a record of the permission that a customer gives you to debit their bank account.

Stripe handles mandate collection as part of the payment flow when you use Checkout, Payment Links, or the Payment Element. The customer reviews the mandate terms and accepts them during the payment process. After the customer authorizes the mandate, Stripe sends a confirmation email to the customer per Nacha compliance requirements.

Mandate types

ACH supports both online and offline mandates:

  • Online mandates — Collected digitally through your payment flow. Stripe manages these automatically when using Checkout, Payment Links, or the Payment Element.
  • Offline mandates — Collected through paper forms or phone calls. If you collect mandates offline, you're responsible for storing the authorization and providing it if a dispute arises.

For detailed information on mandate requirements, verification methods, and compliance considerations, see the Accept a payment guide.

Settlement and timing

ACH Direct Debit is a delayed-notification payment method. After a customer authorizes a payment, it can take up to 4 business days to confirm whether the payment was successful.

How settlement works

When a customer completes an ACH payment, the PaymentIntent status moves to processing. During this window, the bank can reject the debit for reasons such as insufficient funds, an invalid account, or a disabled account. If the payment succeeds, the status changes to succeeded.

Because of the delayed confirmation, avoid fulfilling orders until the payment status reaches succeeded. If a payment fails after being initially accepted, Stripe removes the funds from your Stripe balance.

Bank account verification

Before debiting a customer's account, the bank account must be verified. Stripe supports two verification methods:

  • Instant verification — Uses Financial Connections to verify the account immediately. This is recommended as it reduces fraud and payment failures.
  • Microdeposits — Two small deposits are sent to the bank account and the customer verifies the amounts. This process takes 1-2 business days and customers have a 10-day window to complete verification.

Transaction Identifiers

After a customer completes an ACH Direct Debit payment, the transaction appears on their bank statement. The statement entry shows your statement descriptor, which you can set on the PaymentIntent.

Use a clear, recognizable statement descriptor to help customers identify the payment and reduce the likelihood of disputes. The descriptor can be up to 10 characters for ACH payments.

Get started with ACH Direct Debit

If you use our front-end products, you can enable ACH Direct Debit directly from your payment method settings — no additional integration required. Stripe then automatically determines the most relevant payment methods to display to your customers.

If your integration requires manually listing payment methods, learn how to manually configure ACH Direct Debit as a payment.
