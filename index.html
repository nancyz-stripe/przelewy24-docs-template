Przelewy24 payments

Allow your Poland-based customers to pay via bank redirect.

Overview

Przelewy24 is a Poland-based payment method aggregator that allows customers to complete transactions online using bank transfers and other methods. Przelewy24 provides a way for customers to pay with over 165 banks.

Payment method family Bank redirect
Usability Single-use
Single-use payment methods are those that can only be used once for a payment and cannot be saved for future transactions. After they're used in a payment attempt, they cannot be attached to customers or reused. Learn more

Instant provisional access allows you start accepting payments with this method right away. See the eligibility section for more details.

PayPro, the provider behind Przelewy24 may review your account after activation to confirm eligibility. In addition to meeting the criteria above, your Stripe-linked website must have:

  • A Terms and Conditions page with your refund policy, privacy policy, and company details
  • An About Us page with your Tax ID or VAT number, company address, and product descriptions with pricing

These must appear on dedicated pages — PayPro doesn't accept details placed only in a site footer.

PayPro has the right to suspend or terminate your use of Przelewy24 for breaching the prohibited business categories or failing to meet the requirements listed above.

If your account is suspended, check your Dashboard or the p24_payments capability object for the specific reason. For suspensions related to missing website information, update your site and contact Stripe support to request a re-review.

Refunds

Przelewy24 supports full and partial refunds, and you can issue multiple refunds against a single payment as long as the total refunded amount doesn't exceed the original charge. Refunds are merchant-initiated, which means your refund policy governs when customers are eligible for a refund. You have up to 180 days from the original payment to submit a refund through Stripe.

How refunds work

Submit refunds using the Dashboard or the Refunds API. When you submit a refund, Stripe sends the request through the Przelewy24 network to the customer's bank. The bank credits the refund to the customer's original bank account. You can't cancel a P24 refund after you submit it.

Refunds typically take 5–10 business days to appear on the customer's bank statement. Depending on when you issue the refund, it appears on the customer's statement as either a reversal or a credit. Refunds issued shortly after the original payment might appear as a reversal—the original charge drops off the statement entirely. Later refunds appear as a separate credit.

Track refund status

Check the status of a refund in the Dashboard on the payment details page, or through the Refunds API. Refunds can remain in a pending state while the bank processes the request.

Stripe sends webhook events for refund status changes:

  • refund.updated — The refund succeeded or its status changed.
  • refund.failed — The refund failed. Check the failure_reason field on the Refund object for details.

Disputes

Przelewy24 requires customers to authenticate payments directly with their bank, which significantly reduces the risk of fraud and unrecognized transactions. As a result, P24 doesn't support the standard dispute process—customers can't file a chargeback through their bank, and funds are never withdrawn from your Stripe balance due to a dispute.

If a customer contacts their bank about a Przelewy24 payment, the bank directs them back to you. You can issue a full or partial refund if appropriate.

Transaction Identifiers

After a customer completes a Przelewy24 payment, the transaction appears on their bank statement. The statement entry includes a unique Przelewy24 reference number followed by your statement descriptor, in the format:

/OPT/X/////P24-XXX-XXX-XXX {your statement descriptor}

You can set a custom statement descriptor of up to 14 characters on the PaymentIntent. If you don't set one, the descriptor defaults to "STRIPE POLAND."

The Przelewy24 reference number is available on the PaymentIntent under p24[reference]. You can share this with customers to help them locate the payment on their bank statement.

Get started with Przelewy24

If you use our front-end products, you can enable Przelewy24 directly from your payment method settings – no additional integration required. Stripe then automatically determines the most relevant payment methods to display to your customers.

If your integration requires manually listing payment methods, learn how to manually configure Przelewy24 as a payment.
